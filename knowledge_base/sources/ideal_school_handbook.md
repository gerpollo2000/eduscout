# IDEAL School of Manhattan — Student & Family Handbook 2025-2026

## About IDEAL School
The IDEAL School of Manhattan is an independent, inclusion-focused school serving students from kindergarten through 12th grade. Founded in 2005, IDEAL is committed to the belief that all students can learn together in a shared environment. Our name stands for Individualized, Differentiated Education for All Learners.

## Admission Requirements
- Application form with $75 non-refundable fee
- Current school transcripts (grades 3+)
- Teacher recommendation letters (2 required)
- Student visit day (full day observation and participation)
- Parent interview with admissions team
- Psychological or neuropsychological evaluation (if applicable)
- IEP or 504 plan (if applicable)
- Financial aid application via FACTS (optional, deadline February 1)

## Tuition and Fees (2025-2026)
- Kindergarten through Grade 5: $48,500/year
- Grades 6-8: $51,000/year
- Grades 9-12: $52,500/year
- Registration fee: $2,000 (non-refundable, applied to tuition)
- Technology fee: $500/year
- Field trip fee: $350/year

## Financial Aid
IDEAL School is committed to socioeconomic diversity. Approximately 25% of students receive some form of financial assistance. Aid is need-based and ranges from 10% to 90% of tuition. Contact financialaid@idealschool.org.

## Special Needs Accommodations

### ADHD Accommodations
IDEAL provides comprehensive support for students with ADHD including:
- Preferential seating in all classrooms
- Extended time on tests and assignments (time-and-a-half standard)
- Separate, quiet testing environments available
- Movement breaks built into the daily schedule
- Executive function coaching sessions (weekly, 1:1)
- Daily planner check-ins with advisory teacher
- Reduced homework load when clinically recommended
- Fidget tools permitted in all classrooms
- Standing desks available in every classroom

### Autism Spectrum Accommodations
- Dedicated social skills groups (weekly, grouped by age)
- Sensory-friendly spaces in every building wing
- Visual schedules and transition warnings
- 1:1 aide support available (additional cost: $15,000-$25,000/year)
- Occupational therapy on-site (weekly sessions included in tuition)
- Speech-language therapy on-site (weekly sessions included in tuition)
- Noise-canceling headphones provided
- Modified lunch environment (quiet lunch option)

### Dyslexia Support
- Orton-Gillingham trained reading specialists on staff
- Wilson Reading System available for intensive intervention
- Audiobook and text-to-speech technology provided
- Modified testing formats (oral exams, dictation options)
- Multi-sensory instruction across all subjects

### Physical Disability & Wheelchair Access
- All three buildings are fully wheelchair accessible
- Elevators in every building (key-card access for students)
- Accessible restrooms on every floor
- Adaptive PE program with certified adaptive PE teacher
- Wheelchair-accessible science labs with adjustable tables
- Accessible stage and performance spaces
- School bus with wheelchair lift (routes: Upper East Side, Upper West Side, Midtown)

### Down Syndrome Support
- Individualized learning plans for all academic subjects
- Peer buddy program
- Life skills integration curriculum (grades 6+)
- Speech-language therapy (included)
- Occupational therapy (included)
- Transition planning starting in grade 8

## Dress Code Policy
IDEAL School does not have a formal uniform requirement. Students are expected to dress in clean, weather-appropriate clothing. The following are not permitted:
- Clothing with offensive language or imagery
- Exposed undergarments
- Open-toed shoes in science labs
Students may wear religious headcoverings at all times.

## Attendance Policy
- School hours: 8:15 AM - 3:15 PM (K-5), 8:00 AM - 3:30 PM (6-12)
- More than 15 unexcused absences per year may result in academic consequences
- Late arrivals after 8:30 AM require a parent note or phone call
- Early dismissal requires written parent request submitted by 8:00 AM

## Anti-Bullying Policy
IDEAL maintains a zero-tolerance policy for bullying, harassment, and discrimination. All incidents are investigated within 24 hours. Restorative justice practices are used as the primary intervention. Repeated offenses may result in suspension or expulsion.

## Technology Policy
- 1:1 iPad program (grades K-2)
- 1:1 MacBook program (grades 3-12)
- Assistive technology available for all students with documented needs
- Screen time is monitored and limited during school hours
- Social media use is prohibited on campus during school hours
