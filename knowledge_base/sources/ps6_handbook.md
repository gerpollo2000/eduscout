# PS 6 Lillie D. Blake School — Family Handbook 2025-2026

## About PS 6
PS 6 Lillie D. Blake is a public elementary school in the Upper East Side of Manhattan, serving grades pre-K through 5. Founded in 1869, it is one of the most highly rated public elementary schools in NYC, consistently scoring in the top 5% on state assessments.

## Admission
- Zoned school: Students living in the PS 6 attendance zone have guaranteed admission
- Out-of-zone applicants may apply (limited seats, lottery-based)
- Pre-K: Apply through MySchoolsNYC
- Free breakfast and lunch available for all students

## Tuition and Fees
- Tuition: Free (NYC public school)
- Supplies: Basic supplies provided
- PTA contribution: Voluntary (suggested $1,500/family, but no student is excluded)
- After-school program: $350/month (optional, 3:00-6:00 PM)

## Special Needs & Accessibility

### ADHD Support
- ICT (Integrated Co-Teaching) classes available in most grades
- SETSS pull-out services available
- School psychologist and social worker on staff
- OT (occupational therapy) available for students with IEPs
- Behavioral intervention support
- Sensory breaks built into K-2 schedule

### Physical Accessibility
- Building has elevator access to all floors
- Wheelchair-accessible entrance on 81st Street side
- Accessible restrooms on every floor
- Adaptive physical education available

### Autism Support
- ASD Nest program (structured inclusion model)
- Two teachers per classroom in ASD Nest classes
- Social skills groups
- Visual supports and structured transitions
- Sensory-friendly classroom environments

### Dyslexia
- Reading Recovery program (grade 1)
- Fundations phonics program (K-2)
- Reading specialists on staff
- Parent workshops on supporting readers at home

## School Hours
- Pre-K: 8:20 AM - 2:40 PM
- Grades K-5: 8:20 AM - 2:40 PM (Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Friday)
- Wednesday early dismissal: 8:20 AM - 12:40 PM

## Dress Code
- No uniform required
- Students should wear comfortable, weather-appropriate clothing
- Sneakers required on PE days
- No flip-flops or platform shoes for safety reasons

## Attendance
- NYC DOE requires 90% attendance rate
- Absences of 3+ consecutive days require doctor's note
- Chronic absenteeism (18+ absences) triggers intervention meeting

## Extracurriculars (PTA-funded)
- Chess club
- Art enrichment
- STEM lab
- Dance
- Drama club (spring musical production)
- Soccer and basketball after-school leagues
- Coding with Scratch
- Cost: Most are free or $200-400/semester through PTA
