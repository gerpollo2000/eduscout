# Beacon High School — Student & Family Handbook 2025-2026

## About Beacon
The Beacon School is a public high school in Manhattan's Upper West Side (Lincoln Square area). Founded in 1993, Beacon uses a portfolio-based assessment model rather than traditional exams. It is one of the most sought-after public high schools in NYC with a competitive admissions process.

## Admission
- Application through NYC DOE MySchoolsNYC portal
- Admissions based on: 7th grade report card, attendance record, and a writing sample
- No standardized test required (SHSAT not used)
- Approximately 250 students admitted per year from 3,000+ applicants
- Priority given to District 3 residents

## Tuition and Fees
- Tuition: Free (NYC public school)
- Activity fee: $10/semester
- No technology fee

## Special Needs & Accessibility

### ADHD Support
- Dedicated learning-plan coordinators work with each student
- Small-group instruction available for core subjects
- Counseling and school-psychology services on site
- Extended time available for portfolio submissions
- Executive function support through advisory program
- Organizational tools and planners provided

### Wheelchair & Physical Accessibility
- Building is wheelchair accessible with elevator access to all floors
- Accessible restrooms on every floor
- Modified gym equipment and adaptive PE options
- Transportation support for students with mobility needs

### Dyslexia
- Reading specialists available
- Text-to-speech technology on all school computers
- Modified assessment formats (oral, visual, project-based)

## Arts & Theater Programs
Beacon has a nationally recognized arts program:
- Drama: Full theater program with courses in acting, directing, and stagecraft
- Student-run theater club produces two full shows per year (fall play, spring musical)
- Stage design and technical theater courses available
- Visual arts: Drawing, painting, sculpture, photography, digital art
- Music: Band, chorus, jazz ensemble, music theory
- Dance: Modern dance, choreography
- Arts courses count toward graduation requirements

## Academic Approach
- Portfolio-based assessment (no traditional final exams)
- Emphasis on critical thinking, research, and presentation
- Senior year capstone project required
- Strong emphasis on writing across all subjects

## College Placement
- 95%+ college attendance rate
- Strong placements at: SUNY/CUNY schools, NYU, Columbia, Bard, Wesleyan
- 3 college counselors on staff

## Dress Code
- No uniform required
- Students must dress appropriately for a professional learning environment
- Closed-toe shoes required in labs and gym
