# Stuyvesant High School — Student Handbook & Policies 2025-2026

## About Stuyvesant
Stuyvesant High School is one of nine specialized public high schools in New York City. Founded in 1904, it is widely regarded as one of the top public high schools in the United States. Admission is based solely on the Specialized High Schools Admissions Test (SHSAT).

## Admission Requirements
- Open to all New York City residents entering 9th grade
- Must take the SHSAT (offered once per year, typically October)
- Admission is based entirely on SHSAT score — no interviews, essays, or grades considered
- Approximately 950 students admitted per year from ~30,000 test-takers
- No tuition (public school)

## Tuition and Fees
- Tuition: Free (NYC public school)
- Activity fee: $15/semester
- Technology fee: None
- Textbooks: Provided free
- School supplies: Student responsibility

## School Schedule
- School hours: 7:45 AM - 3:15 PM
- 10-period day with rotating schedule
- Advisory period twice per week

## Special Needs Accommodations

### ADHD Accommodations
Stuyvesant has a dedicated Special-Needs Office that coordinates ADHD accommodations including:
- Extended test time (time-and-a-half with documentation)
- Quiet testing environments in designated rooms
- Individualized learning plans developed with school psychologist
- Counseling services available (school has 12 full-time counselors)
- Priority scheduling for classes with supportive teachers
- Permission to use fidget tools in class
- Modified homework deadlines when clinically recommended
- Organizational coaching sessions available through guidance office

### Physical Disability & Wheelchair Access
The 345 Chambers Street campus is fully wheelchair-accessible:
- All main buildings have elevators, ramps, and wide doorways
- Restrooms and lockers are ADA-compliant
- Accessible science labs with adjustable tables
- Accessible gym facilities (modified PE program available)
- Free school-bus service for students who need it — the bus is equipped with wheelchair lifts
- Contact for accessibility: Call 212-312-4800 and ask for the Special-Needs Office or ADA Coordinator

### Autism Spectrum Accommodations
- School psychologist consultation available
- Quiet spaces available during free periods
- Modified testing environments
- Peer mentoring program
- Social skills support through guidance office

### Dyslexia Support
- Extended time on all assessments
- Text-to-speech software available on school computers
- Audio versions of textbooks upon request
- Modified assignment formats (oral presentations as alternatives)

## Dress Code Policy
Stuyvesant does not have a mandatory uniform. Students are expected to dress appropriately:
- No clothing with profanity, drug references, or discriminatory messages
- Closed-toe shoes required in science labs and gym
- ID card must be visible at all times during school hours
- Hats and headcoverings for religious purposes are always permitted

## Attendance Policy
- Maximum 10 unexcused absences per semester before academic consequences
- 3 unexcused lates = 1 unexcused absence
- Medical absences require a doctor's note upon return
- Extended absence (5+ consecutive days) requires meeting with guidance counselor
